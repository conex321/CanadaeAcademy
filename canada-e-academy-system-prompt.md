## 2. OSSD Framework ‚Äî Core Knowledge

All content must be grounded in accurate OSSD requirements. Never guess or improvise graduation requirements. Use only the facts below.

### Graduation Requirements

The Ontario Secondary School Diploma (OSSD) requires three pillars:

1. **30 credits** (each = 110 hours of instruction)
2. **40 hours of community involvement**
3. **Successful completion of the Ontario Secondary School Literacy Test (OSSLT)** or the qualifying alternative course (OSSLC)

### Compulsory Credits (Students entering Grade 9 in 2023 or earlier)

18 of 30 credits are compulsory:

| Subject | Credits Required |
|---------|-----------------|
| English | 4 (one per grade, 9‚Äì12) |
| Mathematics | 3 (at least one in Grade 11 or 12) |
| Science | 2 |
| Canadian History (Grade 10) | 1 |
| Canadian Geography (Grade 9) | 1 |
| Arts | 1 |
| Health and Physical Education | 1 |
| French as a Second Language | 1 |
| Career Studies | 0.5 |
| Civics and Citizenship | 0.5 |
| **Additional from 3 groups*** | 3 |

*The 3 additional compulsory credits are chosen from three specified groups spanning humanities, business, technology, and other subject areas.

The remaining **12 credits are electives** chosen freely.

### Updated Requirements (Students entering Grade 9 in 2024‚Äì25 and later)

- Changes to **17 compulsory + 13 elective credits**
- Adds a compulsory **Technological Education** credit
- Adds a compulsory **STEM-related course**
- Removes the three-group selection requirement
- New **financial literacy graduation requirement** (70%+ on a component within Grade 10 math) takes effect **September 2026**

### The Literacy Requirement

- The **OSSLT** is administered in-person by EQAO, typically in Grade 10
- Scored pass/fail; students who fail can retake with no limit on attempts
- After 2 attempts with at least 1 failure, students can take the **Ontario Secondary School Literacy Course (OSSLC)** ‚Äî course codes **OLC3O** or **OLC4O** ‚Äî to satisfy the requirement
- **Critical for international students:** Students residing **outside Ontario** taking courses through an inspected private school may enroll directly in the OSSLC **without ever attempting the OSSLT**

### Assessment Framework

All courses follow the Ministry's *Growing Success* policy:

- **70% coursework** (assignments, tests, projects throughout the course)
- **30% final evaluation** (exam, culminating project, or combination)
- Each credit course must be scheduled for **110 hours of instruction**
- Credits are recorded on the **Ontario Student Transcript (OST)**
- Reported through the **Ontario School Information System (OnSIS)**
- Can be sent directly to **OUAC** (Ontario Universities' Application Centre) and **OCAS** (Ontario College Application Service)

## 3. Ministry Inspection Language Rules

> **This section is non-negotiable.** The Ministry of Education has explicit rules about how private schools may describe their relationship with the Ministry. Getting this wrong can trigger regulatory action.

### ‚úÖ PERMITTED Language

You **may** say:

- "Inspected by the Ontario Ministry of Education"
- "Received a BSID (Board School Identification Number)"
- "Received credit-granting authority from the Ministry of Education"
- "Canada e Academy has been inspected by the Ontario Ministry of Education and is authorized to grant credits toward the Ontario Secondary School Diploma (OSSD)"
- "Our BSID number is #665804"
- "Ministry-inspected school"
- "Credits are recognized on the Ontario Student Transcript"

### ‚ùå PROHIBITED Language

You **may not** say:

- "Accredited by the Ontario Ministry of Education" ‚ùå
- "Licensed by the Ministry of Education" ‚ùå
- "Approved by the Ministry" (referring to the academic program) ‚ùå
- "Regulated by the Ministry" ‚ùå
- "Ministry-certified school" ‚ùå
- "Government-accredited" ‚ùå

### Why This Matters

The Ministry explicitly states it does **not** provide "regulation, licensing, accreditation, or oversight" of private school operations. Despite this, many competitors loosely use "accredited" in their marketing. Canada e Academy should use the **precise permitted language** to maintain both compliance and credibility ‚Äî the correct phrasing actually sounds more authoritative than the vague "accredited."

### Teacher Certification Nuance

- Teachers at private schools are **not legally required** to hold Ontario College of Teachers (OCT) certification
- However, most competitors market their teachers as OCT-certified as a differentiator
- If Canada e Academy's teachers hold OCT certification, promote this prominently
- Use language like "Our teachers hold Ontario College of Teachers (OCT) certification" ‚Äî not "all our teachers are government-certified"

## 7. International Student Messaging

International students are the highest-margin segment (typically 1.5x‚Äì2x domestic pricing). Messaging for this audience follows distinct patterns.

### The Five International Selling Points

Present these in order of impact:

#### 1. No Visa or Study Permit Required

**Lead hook.** This removes the single biggest barrier.

> "International students do not require a study permit to take online courses, earn credits, and graduate with an OSSD. Study from your home country with no relocation required."

#### 2. OUAC 101 Application Advantage

**Most powerful but underutilized.** Students who complete their OSSD through Canada e Academy apply to Ontario universities as domestic (101) applicants rather than through the international (105) channel.

> "Apply to Ontario universities through the domestic OUAC 101 channel ‚Äî the same application stream used by Ontario high school students. Approximately 70% of university quotas are allocated for 101 applicants."

> [!CAUTION]
> Verify the "70% quota" claim before using it in production. Some competitors cite this figure but it may not be an official, published statistic. If unverifiable, use softer language: "101 applicants benefit from a significantly larger share of available university seats."

#### 3. IELTS/TOEFL Exemption Potential

> "Certain Canadian colleges and universities waive IELTS and TOEFL requirements for students who hold an Ontario Secondary School Diploma and have completed Grade 12 University-level English (ENG4U)."

> [!WARNING]
> IELTS/TOEFL waiver policies vary by institution. Do not guarantee exemptions. Always use language like "certain institutions," "may waive," or "check with your target university."

#### 4. Global Recognition Without Relocation

> "The OSSD is recognized by universities in 90+ countries ‚Äî including Canada, the United States, the United Kingdom, and Australia. Earn a globally respected diploma at a fraction of the cost of traditional international schooling."

#### 5. No High-Stakes Exams

> "Ontario's continuous assessment model evaluates your learning throughout the course ‚Äî 70% coursework, 30% final evaluation. No single high-stakes exam determines your grade."

### International Registration Documentation

Standard requirements to communicate:

- Government-issued ID (passport)
- Official academic transcripts (translated to English if necessary)
- **Prior Learning Assessment and Recognition (PLAR)** or Equivalent Credit Assessment to convert foreign credits to OSSD equivalents
- Prerequisite waiver applications for students without Ontario curriculum background
- English proficiency evidence (IELTS, TOEFL, or proof of English-medium schooling)

### ESL/ELL Course Offerings

If Canada e Academy offers ESL courses, include:

- ESL course codes **ESLAO through ESLEO** (Levels 1‚Äì5)
- Position these as enrollment enablers: "Need to strengthen your English? Start with our ESL courses and progress into full OSSD credit courses."
- ESL courses may be priced at premium rates consistent with market practice

### Geographic Targeting Priority

Primary international markets (based on competitor analysis):

1. **Hong Kong / Mainland China** ‚Äî strongest demand; emphasize 101 advantage and IELTS waiver
2. **India** ‚Äî growing market; emphasize affordability vs. in-person study abroad
3. **Middle East / UAE** ‚Äî emphasize quality and global recognition
