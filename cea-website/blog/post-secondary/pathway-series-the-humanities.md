# Post-Secondary Pathway Series: The Humanities

Those interested in the humanities and languages are often passionate, critical thinkers who strive to make a positive impact in their society. Whether you find a particular aspect of the humanities interesting, have a natural talent for languages, or are passionate about making a difference, pursuing further education in the arts, humanities, and languages is an excellent option to consider.

## Suggested Courses

Regardless of what particular domain you are looking to study, it is important to be sure you are taking the right courses at the high school level to satisfy prerequisites for your intended programme. For language and humanities programmes, you will need to have taken English throughout high school with strong grades.

- FSF3U — Grade 11 Core French
- FIF3U — Grade 11 French Immersion

## Potential Programmes

The humanities and languages field includes dozens of potential programmes for you to choose from. Some schools offer the option to enter as an undeclared major so you can explore your options further before committing. Another great aspect of humanities programmes is that you can often combine them in a double major or a major/minor degree. Popular programmes include:

Art History, Communication, English, French, Ethics and Political Philosophy, Geography, Greek and Roman Studies, Women's Studies, History, Indigenous Studies, Linguistics, Philosophy, and Religious Studies.

You can search the selection of programmes at [universitystudy.ca](https://www.universitystudy.ca/) for universities or [ontariocolleges.ca](https://www.ontariocolleges.ca/en/programs) for colleges.

## Recommended Post-Secondary Institutions

**Universities:** University of Toronto, McMaster University, Glendon College (York University), University of Ottawa, McGill University, Western University, University of British Columbia.

**Colleges:** Algonquin College, Fleming College, Confederation College, Georgian College, Sheridan College, Centennial College.

## Scholarships and Bursaries

It is no secret that post-secondary study can be expensive, but it is considered an investment towards a brighter future. Scholarships are typically awarded in acknowledgement of academic excellence, while bursaries are allocated based on financial or situational need. For school-specific awards, browse the university website for their dedicated page. For externally offered scholarships, the [Scholarships Canada database](https://www.scholarshipscanada.com/) lists hundreds of searchable opportunities each year.

## Career Opportunities

A Bachelor of Arts in the humanities or languages can lead to many amazing career opportunities, including: Media Relations Officer, Museum Educator, Gallery Director, Content Developer, Human Rights Officer, Archaeologist, Editor, Teacher, Public Administrator, Historian, Speech Language Pathologist, International Development Officer, Heritage Interpreter, and Urban Planner.

Remember that the path you choose is never set in stone. A shift in focus or discipline is just another way of expanding your skill sets and areas of knowledge.
