# Top Grade 12 Elective Courses for Your Application

If you are a Grade 12 student looking to round out your top six courses for university applications, choosing the right elective can make a real difference. A well-chosen elective demonstrates intellectual curiosity, adds breadth to your academic profile, and can even help you discover a new area of interest.

Here are some of our top recommendations for Grade 12 elective credits.

**Important:** The following course options are electives only. While they are all 4U or 4M-level credits that satisfy minimum admission requirements for post-secondary pathways, **your course selections for Grade 12 should be made based on specific programme requirements first.** If you have questions, speak with your guidance counsellor before registering.

## BBB4M -- International Business

Interested in business, international trade, or marketing? [BBB4M](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/bbb4m) covers all of this and more, giving you an excellent foundation for a career in the business world.

## BOH4M -- Business Leadership

If you dream of running your own business, leading a team, or becoming an entrepreneur, [BOH4M](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/boh4m) is the elective for you. This course culminates in a create-your-own-business project.

## CGW4U -- World Issues

Do you keep up with current global events? [CGW4U](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/cgw4u) uses a geographic lens to understand and analyse major events happening in our world, covering cultural, environmental, economic, and geopolitical issues.

## CPW4U -- Canadian and World Politics

Interested in politics or foreign relations? [CPW4U](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/cpw4u) explores how governments operate locally and internationally, making it a great entry point into the political sphere.

## HFA4U -- Food and Nutrition Science

[HFA4U](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/hfa4u) is a course that everyone can benefit from. Learn about what you eat, where food comes from, and how it fuels your body -- all while earning a university-level credit.

## HHS4U -- Families in Canada

[HHS4U](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/hhs4u) examines family relationships from sociological, anthropological, and psychological perspectives. It explores changing trends in family structures and how norms have evolved across Canada.

## HSB4U -- Challenge and Change in Society

Interested in understanding why humans behave the way we do? [HSB4U](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/hsb4u) allows you to study how cultural, social, and behavioural patterns change over time and discusses the impact of social change.

## NDW4M -- Indigenous Studies

[NDW4M](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/ndw4m) explores a wide range of issues and challenges faced by Indigenous communities around the world, including questions of identity, independence, and cultural relations.

## SES4U -- Earth and Space Science

Fascinated by the cosmos? [SES4U](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/ses4u) provides a solid understanding of Earth's geological history and the structure of the universe, combining earth science with astronomy.

## EWC4U -- The Writer's Craft

If you love to write, [EWC4U](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/ewc4u) is designed as a workshop where you can hone your skills and work toward producing a range of written pieces for a portfolio.

## PSK4U -- Kinesiology

[PSK4U](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/psk4u) focuses on the study of human movement and development, exploring the physiological, psychological, and social impact of sports and physical activity.

## Choosing the Right Elective

When selecting an elective, consider the following:

- **Your interests:** Choose something you are genuinely curious about. You are more likely to earn a strong mark in a course that engages you.
- **Your programme requirements:** Make sure your elective does not conflict with any prerequisites or required courses for your intended programme.
- **Your academic profile:** A well-rounded course selection signals intellectual curiosity to admissions committees.
