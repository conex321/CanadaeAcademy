# Post-Secondary Pathway Series: The Social Sciences

Do you love studying human behaviour and interactions? In this instalment of our Post-Secondary Pathway series, we discuss the high school courses future social science majors should be taking, as well as potential post-secondary programmes, institutions, and career paths you might explore!

Individuals who are interested in the social sciences are typically analytical, globally aware critical thinkers who strive to make a positive impact in their society. Whether you find a particular aspect of the social sciences interesting or you are simply passionate about making a difference, following the social sciences pathway may be the right move for you!

## Suggested Courses

Regardless of what particular domain you are looking to study within, it is important to be sure you are taking the right courses at the high school level to satisfy prerequisites for your intended programme. For most social sciences programmes, you will need to have taken English, at least two of the three core sciences (Biology, Chemistry, and Physics), as well as the core math courses (Advanced Functions and Calculus & Vectors).

## Scholarships and Bursaries

It is no secret that post-secondary study can be expensive, but in today's society, it is considered an investment toward a brighter future. Scholarships and bursaries are great ways to ease the financial burden. Generally speaking, scholarships are awarded in acknowledgement of academic excellence, while bursaries are allocated based on financial or situational need.

For externally offered scholarships, [the Scholarships Canada database](https://www.scholarshipscanada.com/) lists hundreds of searchable opportunities each year.

## Career Opportunities

A Bachelor of Social Sciences can lead to many amazing career opportunities. Here are several potential careers social sciences graduates might choose to pursue:

- Psychologist
- Economist
- Journalist
- Political Scientist
- Social Worker
- Human Rights Advocate
- Environmental Worker
- Archaeologist/Anthropologist
- Public Relations Specialist
- Urban Planner

At the end of the day, remember that the path you choose is never set in stone. Consider a shift in focus or discipline as just another way of expanding your skill sets and areas of knowledge.
