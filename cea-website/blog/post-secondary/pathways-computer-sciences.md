# Post-Secondary Pathways: Computer Sciences

Technology is undeniably the way of the future, making coding languages one of the most important second languages you can study. If you are interested in pursuing a career in computer science, you will need strong analytical and problem-solving skills. You should also expect to be continually learning and adapting to new advances in technology throughout your career.

## Suggested Courses

Most computer science programmes expect applicants to have completed two of the three core sciences (Biology, Chemistry, and Physics). You will also need to have taken Computer Science, Advanced Functions, and Calculus and Vectors in Grades 11 and 12. Here is a full list of suggested courses:

**Grade 11:**
- [MCR3U](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/mcr3u) — Grade 11 Functions
- [SCH3U](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/sch3u) — Grade 11 Chemistry
- SPH3U — Grade 11 Physics

**Grade 12:**
- [ICS4U](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/ics4u) — Grade 12 Computer Science
- [MCV4U](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/mcv4u) — Grade 12 Calculus and Vectors
- [MHF4U](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/mhf4u) — Grade 12 Advanced Functions
- [MDM4U](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/mdm4u) — Grade 12 Data Management
- [SCH4U](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/sch4u) — Grade 12 Chemistry
- [SPH4U](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/sph4u) — Grade 12 Physics

## Potential Programmes and Specialties

**Programmes:** Computer Science, Software Engineering, Computer Engineering, Computing Technology, Information Systems, Programming, Mathematics and Computer Science, Business Administration and Computer Science.

**Specialties:** Algorithms, Computer and Internet Security, Mobile Computing, Computer Game Development, Network Computing, Data Science, Management and Entrepreneurship.

You can search programmes at [universitystudy.ca](https://www.universitystudy.ca/) or [ontariocolleges.ca](https://www.ontariocolleges.ca/en/programs).

## Recommended Post-Secondary Institutions

**Universities:** University of Toronto, Western University, University of Waterloo, Carleton University, McGill University, Concordia University, University of British Columbia.

**Colleges:** Algonquin College, George Brown College, Niagara College, Seneca College, Georgian College, Conestoga College, Centennial College.

## Scholarships and Bursaries

Scholarships are awarded in acknowledgement of academic excellence, while bursaries are allocated based on financial or situational need. For school-specific awards, browse the university website. For externally offered scholarships, the [Scholarships Canada database](https://www.scholarshipscanada.com/Scholarships) lists hundreds of searchable opportunities each year. [Tips for filling out scholarship applications can be found here.](https://www.universitystudy.ca/plan-for-university/scholarship-application-tips/)

## Career Options

A computer science degree can lead to many amazing career opportunities, including:

- Software Developer
- Computer Hardware Engineer
- Database Administrator
- Computer Systems Analyst
- Web Developer
- Computer Network Architect
- Information Security Analyst
- IT Project Manager
- Computer and Information Research Scientist
- Cyber Security Developer
- Multimedia Programmer
- Games Developer

At the end of the day, remember that the path you choose is never set in stone. A shift in focus or discipline is just another way of expanding your skill sets and areas of knowledge.
