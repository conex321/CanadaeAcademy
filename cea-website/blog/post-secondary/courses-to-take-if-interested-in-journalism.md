# 8 Courses to Take If You Are Interested in Journalism

Journalism is an exciting and dynamic field that combines writing, research, critical thinking, and communication skills. If you are considering a career in journalism, media, or communications, choosing the right high school courses can give you a strong foundation. Here are eight courses available at [Canada e Academy](https://canadaeacademy.com/) that can help prepare you for a future in journalism.

## 1. ENG4U -- Grade 12 English

English is the backbone of any journalism career. [Grade 12 University English](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/eng4u) develops your reading, writing, and analytical skills -- all essential for producing clear, compelling content. This course is also a prerequisite for virtually every post-secondary programme.

## 2. EWC4U -- Grade 12 Writer's Craft

[Writer's Craft](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/ewc4u) focuses specifically on developing your writing skills across a variety of genres and styles. You will learn how to craft narratives, develop your voice, and refine your writing process -- skills that are directly applicable to journalism.

## 3. HSB4U -- Grade 12 Challenge and Change in Society

Journalists need a deep understanding of social issues. [Challenge and Change in Society](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/hsb4u) explores the factors that lead to social change and encourages students to think critically about the world around them.

## 4. CPW4U -- Grade 12 Canadian and International Politics

Understanding political systems and current affairs is crucial for many areas of journalism. [Canadian and International Politics](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/cpw4u) provides a strong foundation in political theory and practice.

## 5. CGW4U -- Grade 12 Canadian and World Issues

[Canadian and World Issues](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/cgw4u) examines global challenges and how they affect Canada. This course develops the kind of global awareness that is essential for journalists covering international stories.

## 6. CIA4U -- Grade 12 Analysing Current Economic Issues

A solid understanding of economics helps journalists cover business, finance, and policy stories. [Analysing Current Economic Issues](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/cia4u) provides a foundation in economic concepts and current affairs.

## 7. CHY4U -- Grade 12 World History

Understanding the past is essential for understanding the present. [World History Since the 15th Century](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/chy4u) provides the historical context that enriches journalism and helps reporters understand the roots of current events.

## 8. HZT4U -- Grade 12 Philosophy: Questions and Theories

Philosophy develops critical thinking and argumentation skills that are valuable in any form of writing. [Philosophy: Questions and Theories](https://canadaeacademy.com/courses/hzt4u) challenges students to think deeply and articulate complex ideas clearly.

## Building Your Path to Journalism

While these courses provide an excellent foundation, remember that journalism values diverse experiences and perspectives. Consider combining these courses with extracurricular activities such as writing for a school or community publication, starting a blog, or volunteering with local media organisations.

At Canada e Academy, our [guidance team](https://canadaeacademy.com/guidance) can help you plan a course schedule that aligns with your career interests and post-secondary goals.
