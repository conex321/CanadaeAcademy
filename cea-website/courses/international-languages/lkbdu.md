# LKBDU: Simplified Chinese, Level 3

**Grade:** Level 3 | **Credit Value:** 1.0 | **Course Type:** University Preparation
**Prerequisite:** LKBCU (Simplified Chinese, Level 2)

---

## Course Description

This advanced course builds on the skills developed in Level 2 Simplified Chinese, preparing students for university-level language study. Students will refine their listening, speaking, reading, and writing abilities through engagement with complex texts, formal writing tasks, and in-depth cultural analysis. Emphasis is placed on expanding vocabulary for academic and professional contexts, analysing literary and media texts in Simplified Chinese, and developing the ability to express nuanced ideas and opinions with accuracy and fluency.

---

## Course Outline

### Unit 1: Advanced Communication and Formal Register (18 hours)
Students develop the ability to communicate in formal and semi-formal contexts. Topics include academic discussions, formal correspondence, and professional communication in Simplified Chinese.

### Unit 2: Contemporary Chinese Society and Media (18 hours)
Students analyse contemporary Chinese media, including news articles, opinion pieces, and digital content. They develop critical reading skills and learn to summarise, compare, and evaluate viewpoints in Simplified Chinese.

### Unit 3: Chinese Literature and Cultural Heritage (18 hours)
Students explore selections from Chinese literature, including poetry, short stories, and essays. They analyse themes, literary devices, and cultural significance while strengthening their interpretive reading and analytical writing skills.

### Unit 4: Research, Inquiry, and Academic Writing (18 hours)
Students develop academic writing skills in Simplified Chinese, including essay structure, argumentation, and research methodology. They complete a research-based writing project on a topic related to Chinese culture or society.

### Unit 5: Global Perspectives and Cross-Cultural Analysis (18 hours)
Students examine China's role in the global community, exploring topics such as international relations, cultural exchange, and the Chinese diaspora. They develop the ability to discuss complex issues with nuance and cultural sensitivity.

### Unit 6: Review and Culminating Activities (10 hours)
Students consolidate their learning through integrated tasks, including oral presentations, written analyses, and portfolio review.

### Final Evaluation (10 hours)
The final evaluation consists of a written examination and an oral proficiency assessment.
