# LKBBD: Simplified Chinese, Level 1

**Grade:** Level 1 | **Credit Value:** 1.0 | **Course Type:** Academic
**Prerequisite:** None

---

## Course Description

This course introduces students to the Chinese language and culture, developing basic skills in listening, speaking, reading, and writing in Simplified Chinese characters. Students will explore foundational vocabulary, grammar structures, and everyday communicative situations. Through authentic cultural materials, students will also develop an understanding of Chinese customs, traditions, and contemporary society. By the end of the course, students will be able to communicate in simple, everyday contexts using Simplified Chinese.

---

## Course Outline

### Unit 1: Introduction to Mandarin Chinese and Pinyin (18 hours)
Students learn the fundamentals of Mandarin pronunciation using Pinyin, including tones, initials, and finals. Basic greetings, self-introductions, and classroom expressions are introduced alongside an overview of Chinese culture.

### Unit 2: Daily Life and Routines (18 hours)
Students develop vocabulary and sentence structures related to daily activities, telling time, and describing routines. Simplified Chinese character writing is introduced, with a focus on stroke order and basic radicals.

### Unit 3: Family, Friends, and Relationships (18 hours)
Students learn to describe family members and relationships, express likes and dislikes, and discuss hobbies and interests. Reading comprehension of short passages in Simplified Chinese is developed.

### Unit 4: Food, Shopping, and Numbers (18 hours)
Students build vocabulary related to food, dining, shopping, and currency. They practise ordering meals, making purchases, and using numbers in practical contexts.

### Unit 5: Places, Directions, and Transportation (18 hours)
Students learn to describe locations, give and follow directions, and discuss modes of transportation. Cultural topics include Chinese geography and travel customs.

### Unit 6: Review and Culminating Activities (10 hours)
Students consolidate their learning through integrated communicative tasks, cultural presentations, and review activities.

### Final Evaluation (10 hours)
The final evaluation consists of a written examination and an oral communication assessment.
