# LWGBO: German, Level 1

**Grade:** Level 1 | **Credit Value:** 1.0 | **Course Type:** Open
**Prerequisite:** None

---

## Course Description

This course introduces students to the German language and culture, developing basic communication skills in listening, speaking, reading, and writing. Students will learn foundational vocabulary, grammar structures, and everyday expressions needed for practical communication. Through authentic cultural materials, students will explore German-speaking countries, their customs, traditions, and contemporary life. By the end of the course, students will be able to engage in simple conversations and understand short texts in German.

---

## Course Outline

### Unit 1: Getting Started with German (18 hours)
Students learn the German alphabet, pronunciation rules, and basic greetings. Topics include self-introductions, classroom vocabulary, and an overview of German-speaking countries and cultures.

### Unit 2: Daily Life and Routines (18 hours)
Students develop vocabulary and sentence structures related to daily activities, school life, and telling time. Basic German grammar, including noun genders, articles, and simple verb conjugation, is introduced.

### Unit 3: Family, Home, and Community (18 hours)
Students learn to describe family members, their home environment, and their community. Reading and writing skills are developed through short texts, personal descriptions, and informal correspondence.

### Unit 4: Food, Shopping, and Leisure (18 hours)
Students build vocabulary related to food and dining, shopping, and leisure activities. They practise ordering meals, making purchases, and discussing hobbies and pastimes.

### Unit 5: Travel, Directions, and Weather (18 hours)
Students learn to discuss travel plans, give and follow directions, and describe weather and seasons. Cultural topics include geography, festivals, and traditions in German-speaking countries.

### Unit 6: Review and Culminating Activities (10 hours)
Students consolidate their learning through integrated communicative tasks, cultural presentations, and review activities.

### Final Evaluation (10 hours)
The final evaluation consists of a written examination and an oral communication assessment.
