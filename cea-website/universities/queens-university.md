# Pathway to Queen's University

## About Queen's University

Queen's University is one of Canada's oldest and most prestigious degree-granting institutions, founded in 1841 in Kingston, Ontario. Known for its strong sense of community, exceptional student experience, and rigorous academic programmes, Queen's consistently ranks among the top universities in Canada.

The university offers more than 125 programs across faculties including Arts and Science, Engineering and Applied Science, the Smith School of Business, Health Sciences, Education, and Law. With approximately 26,000 students and an extensive alumni network, Queen's is celebrated for its close-knit campus culture, high student satisfaction, and strong graduate employment outcomes.

## How Canada e Academy Students Apply

Canada e Academy students applying to Queen's University use the OUAC system:

- **OUAC 101:** Students currently enrolled at Canada e Academy apply through the 101 stream. Midterm and final marks are submitted directly by Canada e Academy.
- **OUAC 105:** Students who have previously graduated or are applying from outside Ontario use the 105 stream. Official transcripts can be requested from Canada e Academy.

### Key Dates

| Milestone | Typical Deadline |
|---|---|
| OUAC 101 application opens | October |
| Equal consideration deadline | Mid-January |
| Supplementary application (commerce, engineering) | February |
| Offers of admission begin | Mid-February onwards |

*Admission requirements vary by program. Always verify current prerequisites on the [Queen's University admissions website](https://www.queensu.ca/admission).*

## English Proficiency

Students who complete Ontario curriculum English courses (such as ENG4U) at Canada e Academy may have English proficiency requirements waived for admission purposes. However, policies vary by institution and by program. Students should confirm English proficiency requirements directly with Queen's admissions office.
