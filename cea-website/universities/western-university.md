# Pathway to Western University

## About Western University

Western University (officially the University of Western Ontario) is a public research university located in London, Ontario. Founded in 1878, Western is one of Canada's oldest and most respected universities, known for its beautiful campus, strong academic programmes, and vibrant student life. Western is a member of the U15 Group of Canadian Research Universities.

The university offers a full range of programs through its faculties of Arts and Humanities, Education, Engineering, Health Sciences, Information and Media Studies, Law, the Schulich School of Medicine and Dentistry, Music, Science, Social Science, and the Ivey Business School. With approximately 35,000 students and three affiliated university colleges (Brescia, Huron, and King's), Western provides a comprehensive and engaging university experience.

## How Canada e Academy Students Apply

Canada e Academy students applying to Western University use the OUAC system:

- **OUAC 101:** Students currently enrolled at Canada e Academy apply through the 101 stream. Midterm and final marks are submitted directly by Canada e Academy.
- **OUAC 105:** Students who have previously graduated or are applying from outside Ontario use the 105 stream. Official transcripts can be requested from Canada e Academy.

### Key Dates

| Milestone | Typical Deadline |

*Admission requirements vary by program. Always verify current prerequisites on the [Western University admissions website](https://welcome.uwo.ca/admissions).*

## English Proficiency

Students who complete Ontario curriculum English courses (such as ENG4U) at Canada e Academy may have English proficiency requirements waived for admission purposes. However, policies vary by institution and by program. Students should confirm English proficiency requirements directly with Western's admissions office.
