# Pathway to Laurentian University

## About Laurentian University

Laurentian University (French: Universite Laurentienne) is a mid-sized bilingual university located in Greater Sudbury, Ontario, incorporated on March 28, 1960. While primarily focusing on undergraduate programming, Laurentian also houses the east campus of the Northern Ontario School of Medicine (NOSM University), which opened in 2005. Its School of Graduate Studies offers a number of graduate-level degrees.

Laurentian is one of the largest bilingual providers of distance education in Canada, offering programs in both English and French. The university is well known for its programs in mining engineering, Indigenous studies, forensic science, and sports administration. Its northern Ontario location provides unique research opportunities in environmental science, geology, and health sciences.

## How Canada e Academy Students Apply

Canada e Academy students applying to Laurentian University use the OUAC system:

- **OUAC 101:** Students currently enrolled at Canada e Academy apply through the 101 stream. Midterm and final marks are submitted directly by Canada e Academy.
- **OUAC 105:** Students who have previously graduated or are applying from outside Ontario use the 105 stream. Official transcripts can be requested from Canada e Academy.

### Key Dates

| Milestone | Typical Deadline |
|---|---|
| OUAC 101 application opens | October |
| Equal consideration deadline | Mid-January |
| Late applications accepted | On a rolling basis (space permitting) |
| Offers of admission begin | Mid-February onwards |

## English Proficiency

Students who complete Ontario curriculum English courses (such as ENG4U) at Canada e Academy may have English proficiency requirements waived for admission purposes. However, policies vary by institution and by program. Students should confirm English proficiency requirements directly with Laurentian's admissions office.
