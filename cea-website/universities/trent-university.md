# Pathway to Trent University

## About Trent University

Trent University is a public liberal arts university located in Peterborough, Ontario, with a satellite campus in Oshawa serving the Regional Municipality of Durham. Founded in 1964, Trent is known for its Oxbridge-inspired college system and small class sizes that foster close student-faculty relationships and a personalized learning experience.

Trent operates largely through its colleges: Champlain, Lady Eaton, Catharine Parr Traill, Otonabee, Peter Gzowski, and Julian Blackburn. Each college has its own residence halls, dining room, and student government. Although primarily an undergraduate institution, Trent also offers graduate programs at the master's and doctoral levels. The university is consistently ranked among Canada's top undergraduate universities and is especially well regarded in the areas of environmental science, Indigenous studies, forensic science, and the humanities.

## How Canada e Academy Students Apply

Canada e Academy students applying to Trent University use the OUAC system:

- **OUAC 101:** Students currently enrolled at Canada e Academy apply through the 101 stream. Midterm and final marks are submitted directly by Canada e Academy.
- **OUAC 105:** Students who have previously graduated or are applying from outside Ontario use the 105 stream. Official transcripts can be requested from Canada e Academy.

### Key Dates

| Milestone | Typical Deadline |
|---|---|
| OUAC 101 application opens | October |
| Equal consideration deadline | Mid-January |
| Late applications accepted | On a rolling basis (space permitting) |
| Offers of admission begin | Mid-February onwards |

## English Proficiency

Students who complete Ontario curriculum English courses (such as ENG4U) at Canada e Academy may have English proficiency requirements waived for admission purposes. However, policies vary by institution and by program. Students should confirm English proficiency requirements directly with Trent's admissions office.
