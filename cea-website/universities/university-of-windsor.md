# Pathway to the University of Windsor

## About the University of Windsor

The University of Windsor is a comprehensive, student-focused university located in Windsor, Ontario, with more than 16,000 students enrolled in a broad range of undergraduate and graduate programs. The university is known for its strong student-faculty relationships, award-winning faculty, and innovative staff who create a supportive atmosphere of excellence across all faculties.

The University of Windsor offers programs across a wide range of disciplines, including:

- Law
- Business
- Engineering
- Science
- Education
- Nursing
- Human Kinetics
- Social Work
- Arts, Humanities, and Social Sciences

With its location along the Detroit River and proximity to the United States border, the University of Windsor provides unique cross-border educational and research opportunities. The university is committed to encouraging lifelong learning, teaching, research, and discovery.

## How Canada e Academy Students Apply

Canada e Academy students applying to the University of Windsor use the OUAC system:

- **OUAC 101:** Students currently enrolled at Canada e Academy apply through the 101 stream. Midterm and final marks are submitted directly by Canada e Academy.
- **OUAC 105:** Students who have previously graduated or are applying from outside Ontario use the 105 stream. Official transcripts can be requested from Canada e Academy.

### Key Dates

| Milestone | Typical Deadline |
|---|---|
| OUAC 101 application opens | October |
| Equal consideration deadline | Mid-January |
| Late applications accepted | On a rolling basis (space permitting) |
| Offers of admission begin | Mid-February onwards |

## English Proficiency

Students who complete Ontario curriculum English courses (such as ENG4U) at Canada e Academy may have English proficiency requirements waived for admission purposes. However, policies vary by institution and by program. Students should confirm English proficiency requirements directly with Windsor's admissions office.
