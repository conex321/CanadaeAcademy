# Pathway to York University

## About York University

York University is one of Canada's largest universities, located in Toronto, Ontario. Founded in 1959, York has grown into a comprehensive, research-intensive institution with approximately 55,000 students across two main campuses: the Keele campus in north Toronto and the Glendon campus in midtown Toronto. York is well known for its strengths in business, law, liberal arts, environmental studies, health, and fine arts.

The university offers more than 200 programs through 11 faculties, including the Schulich School of Business (one of the top business schools in the world), Osgoode Hall Law School, the Lassonde School of Engineering, the Faculty of Health, the Faculty of Science, and the School of the Arts, Media, Performance and Design. York's commitment to interdisciplinary research and experiential education makes it a dynamic choice for students.

## How Canada e Academy Students Apply

Canada e Academy students applying to York University use the OUAC system:

- **OUAC 101:** Students currently enrolled at Canada e Academy apply through the 101 stream. Midterm and final marks are submitted directly by Canada e Academy.
- **OUAC 105:** Students who have previously graduated or are applying from outside Ontario use the 105 stream. Official transcripts can be requested from Canada e Academy.

### Key Dates

| Milestone | Typical Deadline |
|---|---|
| OUAC 101 application opens | October |
| Equal consideration deadline | Mid-January |
| Supplementary application (Schulich, etc.) | Varies by program |
| Offers of admission begin | Mid-February onwards |

*Admission requirements vary by program. Always verify current prerequisites on the [York University admissions website](https://futurestudents.yorku.ca).*

## English Proficiency

Students who complete Ontario curriculum English courses (such as ENG4U) at Canada e Academy may have English proficiency requirements waived for admission purposes. However, policies vary by institution and by program. Students should confirm English proficiency requirements directly with York's admissions office.
