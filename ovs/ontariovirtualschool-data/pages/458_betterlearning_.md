## Frequently Asked Questions about Online Learning in Ontario

[Open all FAQs](javascript:void(0))

### Can I earn high school credits through online learning in Ontario?

Yes. Ontario Virtual School (OVS) is inspected and accredited by the Ontario Ministry of Education. OVS is authorized to issue Ontario Secondary School Diploma (OSSD) credits. Those credits are identical to those issued by public schools.

All courses follow the Ontario curriculum and count toward your diploma. Report cards and final exams are administered, ensuring academic rigor is maintained.

### Is online learning in Ontario recognized by universities and colleges?

Yes. Universities and colleges throughout Ontario and across Canada accept credits earned through accredited online schools like OVS. Our credits carry the same value as traditional high school credits. Admissions offices evaluate OSSD credits based on performance and accreditation, not delivery method.

### Is Ontario online learning a good fit for different learning styles?

Yes. OVS offers flexibility and self-paced learning that suits a wide range of learning styles. Students who are motivated, can work independently, and have basic computer skills thrive in this format.

In addition, OVS supports students in developing organization, time-management, self-regulation, and collaboration skills. These core learning skills are aligned with diverse approaches to learning. Our teachers are Ontario-certified and provide ongoing support tailored to individual needs.
