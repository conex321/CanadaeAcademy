![Strengthening Global Connections](https://www.ontariovirtualschool.ca/wp-content/uploads/Strengthening-Global-Connections-500x419.png)

Last week marked a significant milestone for [**Ontario Virtual School (OVS)**](https://www.ontariovirtualschool.ca/) as we proudly signed a partnership with [**St. Joseph Gakuen Nissei High School**](https://www.nisseihs.ed.jp/) in [**Kyoto, Japan**](https://kyoto.travel/en/). The signing ceremony took place at the [**Embassy of Canada in Tokyo**](https://www.international.gc.ca/country-pays/japan-japon/tokyo.aspx?lang=eng) on March 28, 2025, in a celebration of international education and cultural exchange.

This new partnership will allow students at Nissei High School to enroll in OVS’s fully online Ontario Secondary School Diploma (OSSD) program, while continuing their local Japanese studies. Upon completion, students will graduate with [**dual diplomas**](https://www.ovsjapan.com/)—both the Japanese high school diploma and the internationally recognized [**OSSD**](https://www.ontario.ca/page/earning-your-high-school-diploma), greatly enhancing their post-secondary options domestically and around the world.

**A Commitment to Global Learning**

At OVS, we believe that education knows no borders. This partnership represents our shared commitment to delivering high-quality, accessible, and globally minded education. By collaborating with outstanding institutions like Nissei High School, we are empowering students with the tools they need to thrive in a globalized world.

**Why the OSSD?**

The Ontario Secondary School Diploma is one of the most respected high school diplomas in the world. Recognized by top universities in Canada, the U.S., the U.K., Australia, and beyond, the OSSD emphasizes critical thinking, communication, and independent learning—skills essential for future academic and career success.

**Looking Ahead**

This partnership is just the beginning of an exciting journey. With Nissei High School’s longstanding tradition of academic excellence and OVS’s innovative virtual learning platform, we are confident this partnership will open doors for students and foster meaningful international collaboration.

We extend our heartfelt thanks to the Embassy of Canada in Tokyo for hosting the ceremony and to the dedicated educators at Nissei High School for their vision and enthusiasm. We look forward to working closely together to inspire the next generation of global leaders.
